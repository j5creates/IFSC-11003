Common Water Questions

Got questions about water? You're in the right place. From the science behind H₂O to practical conservation tips, we've got answers to the most common questions about Earth's most vital resource.

What percentage of Earth's water is actually drinkable?
Only 2.5% of Earth's water is fresh water, and most of that is trapped in glaciers and ice caps. Less than 1% of all water on Earth is readily accessible for human use.
Why does ice float instead of sink?
Water expands when it freezes, making ice less dense than liquid water. This unique property is crucial for aquatic ecosystems, allowing lakes to freeze from the top down and protecting life below.
How much water should I drink daily?
While the "8 glasses a day" rule is popular, actual needs vary by body size, activity level, and climate. A good rule is to drink when thirsty and monitor urine color—pale yellow indicates good hydration.
